      Expert Advice

      Resume Writing Tips

      Tried-and-tested advice from hiring managers and recruiters to make your resume stand out.

      01

      Tailor Every Application

      Customize your resume for each job. Mirror the language in the job description. ATS systems reward keyword alignment — use the exact terms they use.

      02

      Lead with a Strong Summary

      A 2–3 sentence professional summary at the top immediately tells recruiters who you are and what value you bring. Don't skip it — it sets the tone.

      03

      Quantify Your Impact

      Replace vague duties with measurable results. "Increased sales by 34%" beats "responsible for sales." Numbers create instant credibility and memorability.

      04

      Use Action Verbs

      Start every bullet with a strong action verb: Led, Designed, Reduced, Launched, Optimized. Avoid passive language like "was responsible for" or "helped with."

      05

      Keep It to One Page (Usually)

      For under 10 years of experience, aim for one page. For senior roles, two pages are fine. Never pad — every line should earn its place.

      06

      Format for ATS First

      Use standard section headings (Experience, Education, Skills). Avoid tables, columns, and images. Save as PDF. ATS robots parse text sequentially — keep it clean.

      07

      Highlight Transferable Skills

      Changing careers? Identify skills from past roles that apply to the new field. Communication, leadership, project management — these cross every industry.

      08

      Proofread Obsessively

      A single typo can get you rejected. Read it aloud. Use Grammarly. Ask a friend to review it. Then read it backwards, word by word. No errors, ever.
